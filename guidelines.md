Product Requirements Document: Premium Architectural Design Studio Website
1. Introduction
The new website will serve as a “digital showroom” for the architecture studio, showcasing its commitment to luxury, innovation, and sustainability. Inspired by Apple’s branding, the site must be visually striking yet minimal, using large imagery and simple typography to make an emotional connection with visitors. It will educate serious clients on the value of thoughtful design (passive strategies, contextual architecture, net-zero buildings, local materials) while positioning the studio as an aspirational, high-end brand.
Context: An India-based architectural design studio (starting in Bihar) repositioning from local practice to a premium, luxury brand.


Purpose: Attract discerning clients and partners by communicating the firm’s philosophy and expertise in elegant, engaging ways.


Inspirational model: Emulate Apple’s approach – “selling a worldview” of elegance and simplicity rather than technical features, and treating the website as a prestige showcase.


2. Goals & Objectives
Brand Repositioning: Convey a premium, aspirational identity. The site should feel refined and exclusive, so visitors immediately associate the firm with quality and design leadership.


Value Education: Teach clients why good architecture matters. Content will explain how thoughtful design (passive cooling, sustainability, local materials) solves real problems, helping visitors “understand your point of view”.


Strategic Lead Generation: Generate qualified inquiries. Implement local-first SEO (e.g. keywords like “architecture studio Bihar”, “sustainable design India”) and clear calls to action to capture serious leads. (Case studies show targeted SEO can boost architect inquiries by ~175%.)


Showcase Expertise: Present projects with full technical and narrative depth. Each project page should “show, not just tell” – using vivid storytelling and sensory details to immerse readers in the experience of the design.


Trust & Authority: Reinforce credibility. The site will include testimonials and thought-leadership content so potential clients see the firm’s track record and values early, building trust before any contact.


3. Target Audience
Primary: Discerning clients in Bihar (and surrounding regions) who value innovative, sustainable design – e.g. high-income homeowners, developers, institutions. These clients expect quality and are willing to invest in premium architecture.


Secondary (later): National audience across India with similar values (tech-savvy, environmentally conscious, design-oriented).


Audience needs: Information on how the firm’s approach meets their aspirations. They research online first, so the site must clearly answer why choose us – what unique value the firm brings (e.g. climate-responsive design, cultural sensitivity).


4. Brand Positioning & Messaging
Philosophy: Emphasize a creative, visionary outlook. Use storytelling and imagery to position the firm as a thought leader, not just a service provider.


Tone and Voice: Sophisticated, confident, and educational. Copy should use precise, evocative language (e.g. “breathable spaces”, “timeless harmony”) rather than jargon. Reflect Apple’s approach: focus on lifestyle and emotion (e.g. “You’ll feel at home under clean natural light” rather than technical specs).


Tagline/Headline: A short, clear headline on the homepage is essential. RIBA notes architects often waste time on an uncaptioned hero image, so we will include a compelling tagline (e.g. “Architecture for Sustainable Living”) immediately visible. This tagline should encapsulate the studio’s unique value and filter in ideal clients.


Visual Branding: Clean and minimalist. Use a restrained color palette (mostly white/neutral with one accent color). Typography should be modern and legible; whitespace and grid layouts will convey elegance. Consistent, high-quality photos and graphics reinforce the luxury feel.


5. Key Features and Sections
5.1 Homepage (Immersive Hero & Overview)
Hero Section: Full-bleed high-resolution image or short looped video of a signature project, overlaid with the studio’s brief slogan and a concise subheading of its mission. The headline must immediately convey the brand’s essence. (For example, Louis Vuitton’s site uses “striking visuals and video, creating a memorable experience” with minimal overlay text.)


Clear Navigation: A visible menu (e.g. top bar with “About | Projects | Strategies | Contact”). RIBA advises against hiding the menu behind images or requiring visitors to hunt for it. Ensure navigation is intuitive and persistent.


Intro Snapshot: Just below the fold, a short statement about the firm (“Studio Aashraya is an architecture practice dedicated to sustainable, contextual design in India”) in clean typography. This anchors the brand and complements the hero.


Featured Highlights: A brief overview or “pillars” segment that hints at the studio’s core strengths (e.g. “Net-Zero Design, Passive Strategies, Vernacular Materials”). Each item here can link to more detail.


Call to Action: A prominent button such as “Explore Our Work” or “Request Consultation”. Use action-oriented text that aligns with a high-end voice (“Envision Your Project”, “Begin Your Journey”, etc.).


5.2 Featured Strategies (Design Philosophy)
Section Title: Featured Strategies or Our Approach. This section clearly articulates the firm’s signature methods.


Content Blocks: Divide into cards or columns (with icons or images) for each strategy, for example:


Passive Design: Explain how projects use natural ventilation, daylight, and shading to eliminate the need for air-conditioning – “No mechanical cooling required thanks to passive climate strategies”.


Net-Zero Buildings: Highlight renewable energy integration. (E.g. “Our buildings harness solar power on-site to meet 100% of their energy needs,” as exemplified by India’s first net-zero library.)


Contextual Design: Emphasize climate and culture. (Note: Bahuarwa School in Bihar “respects local traditions” and environment in its design.)


Vernacular & Local Materials: Stress use of bamboo, earth, brick, and other region-specific materials to reduce footprint and harmonize with place. (The Jhansi Library case shows success with “locally-sourced and recycled materials” for net-zero projects.)


Brief Descriptions: For each, 2–3 sentences and a “Learn More” link. Use plain language to educate (no heavy jargon). Infographics or subtle animations can illustrate benefits (e.g. a graphic showing sun path or airflow).


Outcome Messaging: Convey the benefits – e.g. “These strategies cut energy costs and honor Bihar’s heritage,” implying both practical and cultural value.


5.3 Project Showcase (Portfolio)
Projects Index: A gallery or grid of key projects with attractive thumbnails and brief titles. Include filters/categories (Residential, Commercial, Education, etc.). Navigation should be smooth (e.g. infinite scroll or pagination).


Individual Project Pages: Each project must have:


Hero Image: A stunning full-width photo or render.


Project Overview: Project name, location, date, type.


Story/Narrative: A descriptive paragraph that “shows” the project’s atmosphere and goals. For instance, instead of “This is a café by the hills,” say “Nestled on a terraced hillside, the café opens its glass walls to golden sunset light, inviting visitors to experience the landscape as they dine.” The RIBA guidance highlights using sensory details and storytelling to make the project come alive.


Technical Details: Facts and figures (size, materials, passive features, energy performance) in a clean sidebar or tab. Use bullet lists or icons (e.g. solar panels icon). Keep this concise but precise.


Gallery: A carousel or masonry grid of images (interiors, exteriors, plans). Ensure each has alt-text for accessibility. High-quality photography is a must, reflecting Apple’s standard of “crisp, consistent, and minimalist” imagery.


Testimonial/Quote (optional): If available, include a client quote on project impact to reinforce credibility.


Call-to-Action: At page end, prompt the user to “Discuss Your Project” or similar.


Interactivity: Consider subtle interactive elements (e.g. hover zoom on images, scroll-triggered transitions) that signal sophistication without being gimmicky. The experience must feel polished, reinforcing “luxury” at every click.


5.4 SEO & Lead Generation
On-Page SEO: Each page must have optimized titles, headings, and meta-descriptions rich in target keywords (e.g. “Passive House Design Bihar,” “Net-Zero Architecture India”). The content itself (especially the Project Showcase and Strategies pages) will naturally include these terms.


Local SEO: Create a Google Business Profile for the Bihar office, and ensure NAP (Name/Address/Phone) consistency. Mention the studio’s location(s) and regional context in the About and Contact pages to reinforce local relevance.


Content Strategy: Include a brief news or blog section (even a “News & Insights” page) with articles on sustainability trends, project news, and local design issues. This fresh content helps SEO and educates clients (e.g. “How Monsoon Climate Shapes Our Designs in Bihar”).


Lead Capture: Every page (especially Home and Projects) should have clear CTAs (“Get in touch to learn more”) linking to the contact form. According to marketing best practice, align this with a strong offer (free consultation or site visit).


Analytics & Tracking: Integrate Google Analytics and Search Console to monitor organic traffic, user behavior, and conversion rates. Track metrics like form submissions and newsletter sign-ups. (As an example, one case study showed an architectural firm’s organic leads triple after content optimization.)


5.5 Contact Page (Premium Touchpoint)
Design Consistency: The contact page must match the site’s elegant style. Use the same fonts, colors, and layout conventions as other pages.


Contact Form: Embed a sleek form (e.g. via Typeform or a custom form) that feels purposeful. Form fields should be straightforward but collect enough detail (name, email, project type, message). The submit button should be prominently styled. A short headline above the form (e.g. “Let’s Begin Your Project”) reinforces brand tone.


Additional Info: Include physical address, phone number, and email (with minimalist icons). If appropriate, embed a small map. Keep the section uncluttered.


Encouragement & Trust: A sentence like “We respect your privacy: your information is safe with us” can accompany the form. A note that the firm replies personally to all inquiries emphasizes attentiveness.


Guidelines: Per design best practices, the page should “fulfill its purpose” easily and ideally “delight” the visitor. Avoid any distracting graphics; the focus is on conversion. A subtle success message (“Thank you – we’ll be in touch”) confirms submission graciously.


6. Content and Navigation Guidelines
Site Navigation: A simple top menu with clear labels (e.g. Home, About, Projects, Strategies, Contact). On mobile, use a hamburger menu icon. RIBA guidance warns against hiding navigation behind images or unusual widgets – the menu must be obvious and easy to access.


Information Architecture: The structure should be flat enough that any page can be reached within two clicks from the home. For example: Home → Projects → [specific project]. All main pages (Projects, Strategies, About, Contact) should appear in the primary menu.


Copy Tone: Use first-person plural (we/our) to sound confident and collaborative. Sentences should be brief and active. Bullet lists and headings break up text. According to branding best practices, the site should “use few words and big visuals, clear focus”, avoiding clutter.


Storytelling: Leverage narrative throughout. As one expert notes, merely showing images isn’t enough – incorporate explanations of “how your process connects to their mission”. For example, project pages will detail why each design decision was made, not just what it is.


Testimonials and Social Proof: Sprinkle short quotes from satisfied clients or awards in sidebars or footers to lend credibility. Social proof “brags on your behalf” and is powerful.


7. Visual Design & Branding
Aesthetic: Embrace a minimalist layout with generous whitespace and clean lines. This mirrors Apple’s signature style of “stripping away unnecessary elements”. Every element on the page (button, photo, headline) must look purposeful.


Imagery: Use only professional-quality photos/renders. Imagery should be consistently edited (e.g. similar lighting style). As Apple’s branding shows, consistent, high-end visuals build trust.


Color & Typography: A monochrome or grayscale base palette with a single accent color (e.g. deep blue or warm earthy tone). Typography: one elegant serif or sans-serif for headings and one for body text, both with good legibility. Large, bold fonts for headings convey confidence.


Animations/Effects: Subtle transitions (fade-ins, parallax) can add polish. Avoid heavy animations that slow load time. Apple’s approach avoids gimmicks in favor of “ruthless integration and relentless UX prioritisation”, so all effects must enhance, not distract.


8. Technical Requirements
Responsive: Full mobile responsiveness is mandatory. The layout should adapt gracefully to smartphones/tablets, with touch-friendly controls (e.g. swipeable project galleries).


Performance: Optimize for fast loading: compress images, enable lazy loading for below-the-fold content, and use a modern hosting/CDN. Slow or janky pages would hurt the premium feel and SEO.


Accessibility: Ensure WCAG 2.1 AA compliance – high color contrast, alt text for images, semantic HTML. A luxury brand should be inclusive.


CMS & Tools: Use a flexible CMS (WordPress, Webflow, or similar) that allows easy editing of projects and content. Install SEO plugins/tools for analytics. Include SSL and standard security measures.


Analytics: Integrate Google Analytics and set up goal tracking on lead forms. Regularly review SEO performance and user engagement to iterate the strategy.


9. Success Metrics
Traffic & Rankings: Achieve top-3 Google rankings for key local terms (e.g. “Bihar sustainable architect”) within 6–12 months. Increase organic traffic by ≥100% as measured by analytics.


Engagement: Low bounce rate (<50%) on project pages and average time-on-page ≥2 minutes, indicating deep interest.


Leads: Track form submissions. Aim for a steady stream of qualified inquiries (benchmarked to local market expectations). The site’s conversion should eventually justify claiming it as “a powerful lead-generation engine,” as many firms have achieved.


10. Summary
This PRD envisions a bold, minimalist website that feels like a luxury gallery for architecture. It blends Apple-inspired simplicity and emotional storytelling with evidence-based content on sustainability and design value. By combining immersive visuals and clear messaging, it will reposition the studio as a high-end, trusted authority – ultimately educating clients and generating quality leads. Every element of copy, design, and flow is chosen to reinforce the firm’s expertise and passion for sustainable architecture.

These ensure the site not only looks premium but also effectively communicates the firm’s unique vision and value.
